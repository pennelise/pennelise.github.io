Contributions to Wisconsin Horizontal Interpolation Program for Satellites (WHIPS)

WHIPS is an open-source Python package which regrids level-2 satellite data (irregular overlapping polygons) to level-3 data (gridded data). It supports a number of satellite products including NASA OMI NO2 and MOPPIT CO. I added a new regridding function based on the OMNO2d gridding algorithm, and the ability to read in KNMI GOME-2 NO2 data.
Link to the WHIPS github.

Predicting wind plant power production

Wind farm operators need to predict their power production 24 hours in advance so that they can submit accurate predictions to the grid operators. However, these predictions are imperfect, and the wind industry loses over $300 million each year to inaccurate power forecasts. Wind turbine power production depends in part on the amount of wind shear and turbulence in the surrounding atmosphere. However, these effects are not well understood and can be highly dependent on local conditions. We used a random forest model to incorporate shear and turbulence, as well as local information like altitude, to predict power production at a wind farm. We found that the new model, which incorporated more localized atmospheric information, outperformed the standard industry wind curve.

Penn, E. and Newman, J. A New Toolbox for Operational Wind Data. (2017) Presentation. American Meteorological Society Annual Meeting 2017. Seattle, WA.Link

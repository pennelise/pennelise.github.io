Research Interests

I am a third-year PhD student working with Prof. Daniel Jacob at Harvard University. Broadly, I am interested in using satellite observations with atmospheric chemistry models to better understand what drives changes in air pollutants and greenhouse gasses. My current project uses a global inversion of methane observations in the shortwave infrared and thermal infrared ranges to separately constrain methane emissions and tropospheric OH.

Before graduate school, I worked with Prof. Tracey Holloway at University of Wisconsin studying the diurnal cycle of nitrogen oxides with satellite observaitons. I also worked at the NOAA Geophyisical Fluid Dynamics Lab in summer of 2017 with Prof. Larry Horowitz and Dr. Vaishali Naik validating global NO2 in the GFDL-AM3 model, and at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory in summer 2016 with Dr. Jennifer Newman studying atmospheric effects on wind turbines.
